Technology has advanced so much since the internet was initially created, and because of this more jobs and opportunities to work in this field have appeared.

However, working in technology is one of the most competitive fields in the world, and in order to enter such a densely populated field, one has to be creative and talented. One has to think outside of the box.

Technology

By thinking outside the box, we have effectively destroyed what the internet used to be. We have turned something as simple as a blog page or e-commerce site into a mind game, impossible to navigate through, with laggy animations all over the place. People have replaced usability with aesthetics.

We also see pages that are too boring, that lose the true essence of what the internet used to be. Something fun, fresh, and new.

People found new ways of coding, and coded more creatively, and because of that, we lost the ways in which websites were originally created.

I believe that this article was insightful, looking into the original ways of creating a site, and how different minds have thought up new ways of doing things. It reminds me of looking up how to add a specific HTML or CSS element while I am coding, and finding a blog post/thread where someone starts off with “You can’t technically do this in CSS but…” and gives a solution to your issue.

I think when it comes to coding, I really want all of my elements to look perfectly aligned, and the original ratio/dimensions always look great, and once I try to make it responsive, I am dissatisfied with my original product. I think this article showed me that websites/coding languages were not built with the intention of catering to my heavily CSS-ed sites, and that is why I can never make everything look exactly how I want it. So the next time something isn’t exactly how I like it, I need to just remember that.
